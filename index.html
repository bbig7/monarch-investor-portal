Three Pillars of Structural Edge

The Monarch Engine does not predict markets. It identifies the conditions under which institutional participants are compelled to act, and positions capital in alignment with their structural footprint — before the move, not after.

PILLAR · I

Capital Preservation as the Primary Objective

Every strategy parameter — position sizing, correlation limits, session filters — is designed to protect the base before pursuing return. The Slope Gate enforces a dynamic halt mechanism: when the EMA coefficient drops below the θ-minimum, the engine suspends execution entirely. Drawdown is a mathematical inevitability; the magnitude of drawdown is a design choice.

MAX RISK PER TRADE: 0.5% AUM · MONTHLY HALT: −6%
PILLAR · II

Asymmetric Return Architecture

The engine is calibrated to never accept a trade with a risk-reward ratio below 1:2.0. In practice, the average realised R:R is 1:2.8 — meaning the strategy generates positive expectancy even at win rates below 40%. Paired with an 87.3% win rate on ICS ≥ 7.5 signals, the mathematical edge compounds non-linearly. Every losing trade is a calculated concession; every winning trade is a structural harvest.
